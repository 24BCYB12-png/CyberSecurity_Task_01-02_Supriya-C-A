## Key Learnings

- The **CIA Triad** (Confidentiality, Integrity, Availability) is the foundation that almost every security control, policy, and incident response decision maps back to.
- Real-world breaches (e.g., Colonial Pipeline) usually trace back to a single weak link — in that case, one compromised password without multi-factor authentication.
- Risk assessment is about prioritization: not every asset needs the same level of protection, and impact + likelihood together determine where to focus effort first.
- Most threats (phishing, weak passwords, unpatched systems, insider threats, malware) are preventable with consistent basic hygiene — MFA, patching, training, and backups go a long way.
- Good cybersecurity advice to a non-technical audience (like a college administration) needs to be clear, prioritized, and tied to business impact — not just a list of tools.
